Artificial Intelligence & Higher Education

Relevance and Responsible Innovation

Explore Programs

Objectives

To create a forum for strategic dialogue on AI-higher education nexus among academia, industry, and government to align institutional AI initiatives with national policy frameworks and missions.

To pinpoint key research and innovation priorities for AI-enabled higher education and research, focusing on national and regional development challenges in sectors including agriculture, healthcare, industry, disaster risk reduction, environmental sustainability and climate change resilience.

To raise awareness and draft institutional guidelines for the responsible, ethical, and transparent use of AI in teaching, learning, and research.

To formulate concrete, actionable roadmaps for strengthening institutional capacity in AI-enabled higher education to support long-term preparedness for technological transitions and economic transformation.

Key Themes / Topics

Topics to be covered

1. Curriculum Transformation for an AI-First World

  • Reimagining pedagogy to integrate AI literacy across all disciplines.
  • Developing new interdisciplinary programs at the intersection of AI and other sciences.
  • Addressing the "employability crisis" by equipping graduates with job-ready AI skills.

2. Building Robust Research Infrastructure

  • Strategies for developing and accessing high-performance computing (HPC) clusters.
  • Leveraging national initiatives like the IndiaAI Dataset Platform for data-driven research.
  • Securing sovereign AI capabilities by building indigenous foundation models.

3. Governance and Ethics of AI

  • Drafting institutional policies on the responsible use of AI in teaching and research.
  • Addressing issues of academic integrity, plagiarism, and intellectual property in an AI era.
  • Promoting ethical frameworks for AI development tailored to the Indian context.

4. Forging Academia-Industry-Government Partnerships

  • Creating a seamless pipeline for collaborative projects and research funding.
  • Aligning academic research with industry needs and national development goals.
  • Sharing best practices for technology transfer and commercialization of AI innovations.

Expected Outputs

Tangible outcomes the seminar will produce

1. Draft Roadmap for Institutional AI Readiness

A document outlining actionable steps for universities to prepare their curriculum, research, and governance frameworks for the AI transition.

2. Policy Brief on Responsible AI in Higher Education

A set of recommendations for policymakers on developing a national framework for AI ethics and governance in academia.

3. Published Report

A comprehensive report summarizing the seminar’s proceedings, key discussions, and final recommendations for wider dissemination.

4. Formation of a Collaborative Network

The establishment of a network of academic and industry leaders to continue the dialogue and execute the proposed roadmap.

Rationale

Artificial Intelligence and Higher Education:

Relevance and Responsible Innovation

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly emerging as a transformative force across education, research, industry, and governance. Globally, universities and research institutions are reorienting their curricula, research priorities, and governance frameworks to address both the opportunities and the risks associated with AI. In India, this transformation is being accelerated by landmark initiatives such as the IndiaAI Mission (2024), with an outlay of ₹10,371.92 crore, the Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF) Act, 2023, and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. Together, these policy frameworks aim to strengthen the national research ecosystem, build human capacity, foster innovation, and promote the responsible adoption of new technologies. Complementing these, the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP) establishes the ethical and regulatory foundation necessary for data-driven research that underpins AI applications.

For higher education institutions in India, engaging with AI is both an urgent necessity and a strategic opportunity. Universities play a pivotal role in preparing the next generation of researchers, professionals, and policymakers who must be equipped with AI and data literacy. At the same time, higher education institutions serve as incubators for research and innovation, where AI can be applied to address critical challenges of good jobs creation; food and nutritional security; health care and educational insecurities and inequalities; and climate change and environmental sustainability. An efficient and effective governance and development delivery system is an imperative. By embedding AI within academic programs and research pipelines, universities can contribute not only to advancing knowledge but also to generating localized, context-specific solutions that directly benefit balanced economic and social development without compromising nature that underpins it all.

Against this backdrop, the seminar aims to provide a timely and strategic platform for academic leaders, faculty, students, policymakers, and industry experts to engage in dialogue on Artificial Intelligence and Higher Education. Such a forum enables collective reflection on institutional readiness, identification of priority areas for research and innovation, and the drafting of actionable roadmaps for capacity-building. By bridging the gap between national strategies and institutional implementation, higher education institutions can position themselves to contribute meaningfully to the national AI mission and its goals of innovation, capacity-building, and responsible adoption.
